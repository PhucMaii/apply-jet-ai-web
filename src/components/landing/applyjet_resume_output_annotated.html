Eyes sweep left to right across the top, then drop down the left edge — the F-pattern. The first word of every bullet must be strong.
Name + role line — 0 to 1 second
The only section guaranteed to be read. The subtitle "Senior Full-Stack Developer · 5 Years" tells HR seniority and specialty instantly — before they read a word of history. No subtitle = wasted first impression.
3-line summary — your entire pitch
Most people skip this. Big mistake. If the header hooked them, this is what seals it. Lead with years + your single biggest win (200K users, 38% faster). Then tools. 3 lines max — any longer and it becomes a paragraph nobody reads.
Numbers lead every bullet
See the blue numbers? 38%, 200K, 60%, 50K. HR can't verify them — but they signal credibility instantly. Bullets that start with a metric get read. Bullets that don't, get skipped. "Rebuilt checkout" is invisible. "Cut load time 38%" is not.
Keywords pass the ATS filter first
The green words are keywords matched from the job description. ATS software scores your resume before a human sees it. React, TypeScript, Node.js — if they're in the posting but not your resume, you're auto-rejected. ApplyJetAI mirrors the job's exact language.
Oldest role fades — recency is everything
The 2019 role gets 1 bullet, lower opacity. HR barely reads past 5 years. Give your current role the most bullets (4), older roles progressively fewer. Don't bury your biggest impact under ancient history.
The 1-page rule
Under 7 years exp → 1 page, no exceptions. Every extra line you add forces HR to work harder — and they won't. They'll stop reading. A longer resume signals you can't prioritise. ApplyJetAI enforces this automatically.
